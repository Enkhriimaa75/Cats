How to Take Care of Your Cat

Essential tips to keep your cat happy and healthy

🍖 Feeding Your Cat

  • Feed your cat high-quality cat food appropriate for their age (kitten, adult, or senior)
  • Provide fresh, clean water at all times in multiple locations
  • Adult cats should eat 2-3 times per day on a regular schedule
  • Avoid giving them chocolate, onions, garlic, grapes, raisins, and raw dough
  • Treats should make up no more than 10% of their daily calorie intake
  • Monitor your cat's weight and adjust portions as needed
  • Consult your vet for specific dietary recommendations

🧼 Grooming

  • Brush long-haired cats daily to prevent matting and hairballs
  • Short-haired cats need brushing once or twice a week
  • Trim nails every 2-3 weeks using cat-specific nail clippers
  • Clean ears gently when they look dirty using cotton balls and ear cleaner
  • Brush teeth regularly (ideally daily) to prevent dental disease
  • Bathe your cat only when necessary, as most cats groom themselves
  • Check for fleas, ticks, and skin issues during grooming sessions

🏥 Health Care

  • Take your cat to the veterinarian at least once a year for check-ups
  • Keep all vaccinations up to date as recommended by your vet
  • Use flea and tick prevention treatments year-round
  • Watch for signs of illness such as changes in eating, drinking, or behavior
  • Spay or neuter your cat to prevent health problems and overpopulation
  • Maintain parasite prevention (worms, heartworm)
  • Keep emergency vet contact information easily accessible
  • Consider pet insurance for unexpected medical costs

🎈 Exercise and Play

  • Play with your cat for at least 15-20 minutes twice daily
  • Provide various toys like balls, feather wands, and puzzle feeders
  • Install cat trees or shelves for climbing and vertical space
  • Rotate toys regularly to keep them interesting and engaging
  • Interactive play strengthens your bond and prevents obesity
  • Provide scratching posts to satisfy natural scratching behavior
  • Use laser pointers sparingly and always end with a catchable toy

🏠 Creating a Safe Home

  • Keep toxic plants (lilies, poinsettias, aloe) out of reach
  • Secure windows and balconies to prevent falls
  • Provide clean litter boxes (one per cat plus one extra)
  • Scoop litter boxes daily and change litter weekly
  • Create hiding spots and quiet areas where your cat can feel safe
  • Keep household chemicals, medications, and cleaners locked away
  • Remove small objects that could be swallowed
  • Ensure all electrical cords are hidden or protected

❤ Love and Attention

Cats need emotional care too! Spend quality time with your cat every day, whether through play, grooming, or simply sitting together. Learn to read their body language so you know when they want attention and when they need space. Every cat has a unique personality, so get to know what makes your cat happy.

Respect your cat's boundaries and never force interaction. Some cats are more independent, while others are very affectionate. Provide enrichment through window perches, interactive toys, and safe outdoor experiences like a catio or leash training if they enjoy it.

With proper care, love, and attention, your cat can live a long, healthy, and joyful life as your beloved companion. The average cat lives 12-18 years, but with excellent care, many cats live well into their twenties!
